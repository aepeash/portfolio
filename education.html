My Education Experience

Undergrad

For me the four years I spent in undergrad were very formative, within these four year I did a lot of self discovery and growth. When I started undergrad I had a plan to peruse a degree in Heath Sciences with a want to enter into pharmacy school after graduation. A few weeks into undergrad I changed my major to business, leading to some very long conversations with my mom about her disappointment in this decision. Besides my moms disappointment following a path into business was one of the best decisions I have made. I decide to peruse a degree in Management Information Systems. I soon discovered the cross section between people and technology. Throughout undergrad I did not know what career path I wanted to follow, I went between project management, consulting, data analyst, and security. To try and find my ideal career I worked four different internships. These included working as a administrative intern, help desk intern, a junior system administrator, and a data analyst intern. The most valuable lessons I learned from these work experiences is that company cultural is important and I highly enjoy working with people and not just at a desk all day. Also, I am not a data person. Last during my time at Oakland I was a part of Student ambassadors for the School of Business, Association of Information Systems, and women in Business.

Grad School

Currently I am on my journey of Graduate School. I am at the University of Michigan School of Information focusing on Human Computer Interaction. The adjustment to grad school has been tough, but the SI community has been great at providing support for students. While at SI I've been able to do a lot of engaged learning, which has really helped my growth as a student. I have also been able to learn a lot about user experience and the importance of empathizing with users. This summer I am going to Cape Town South Africa to work with a non-profit which works to help people keep their land.

Work Experience

I feel a big part of my educational experience has been been working internships. In undergrad I worked four different internships, I feel what I apply in grad school was learned through real world work experiences. I've worked as a adminstrative intern, a help desk intern, a system admin intern, and a data analytics intern.
